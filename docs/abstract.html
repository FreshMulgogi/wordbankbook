Variability and Consistency in Early Language Learning: The Wordbank Project

6 Abstract

This chapter focuses on splitting vocabulary data into syntactic categories and analyzing consistency and variability across languages in the acquisition of these. We quantify the “noun bias” across languages. In addition, we report the degree of bias for or against verbs and closed-class words.1

  1. An earlier version of this work was reported to BUCLD 2015 by Braginsky, Marchman, Yurovsky, & Frank.↩
